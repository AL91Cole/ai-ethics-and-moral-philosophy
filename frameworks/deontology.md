# Deontology and AI

Deontology is an ethical framework that focuses on duties, rules, and moral obligations rather than consequences alone.

## Core Idea

From a deontological perspective, some actions are wrong even if they produce useful outcomes. People must be treated as ends in themselves, not merely as tools.

## How It Applies to AI

Deontology raises important questions such as:
- Does this AI system respect human rights?
- Does it preserve informed consent?
- Does it treat people as persons rather than as data objects?
- Does it follow clear moral rules?

## Example

An AI surveillance system may improve public safety, but a deontological analysis would still ask whether it violates privacy, consent, or civil liberties.

Even if the system creates social benefits, it may still be morally wrong if it disrespects human dignity or ignores basic rights.

## Strengths

Deontology is strong in areas where rights and duties matter most, such as:
- privacy
- consent
- fairness
- non-discrimination
- respect for persons

## Weaknesses

A weakness of deontology is that strict rules can sometimes make it harder to resolve situations where moral duties conflict.

For example, an AI system might protect privacy but reduce safety, or increase safety while limiting autonomy.

## Conclusion

Deontology is important in AI ethics because it reminds us that not everything should be justified by efficiency or social benefit. Moral limits still matter.
