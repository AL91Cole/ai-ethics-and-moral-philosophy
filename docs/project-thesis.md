# Project Thesis

This repository explores a central claim: artificial intelligence is not the primary moral agent in AI ethics. The deeper ethical issue is human power.

AI systems do not have human moral understanding, intentions, or accountability. They reflect the goals, assumptions, priorities, and limits of the people and institutions that build and deploy them. Because of that, ethical responsibility must remain with humans.

This repository argues that debates about AI ethics should focus less on treating AI as an independent moral threat and more on questions of governance, accountability, transparency, justice, and fair access.

Main themes include:
- moral agency
- accountability
- algorithmic injustice
- trust
- empathic AI
- social justice
- human dignity
- power and institutional control
