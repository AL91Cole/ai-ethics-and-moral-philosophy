# Core Concepts in AI Ethics

AI ethics studies the moral questions created by artificial intelligence systems and the people who design, deploy, regulate, and use them.

## What AI Ethics Covers

AI ethics asks whether AI systems are being built and used in ways that are fair, safe, accountable, transparent, and respectful of human dignity.

It is not only about whether AI works. It is also about whether AI should be used in a particular way, who benefits, who is harmed, and who is responsible when something goes wrong.

## Key Concepts

### Fairness
Fairness asks whether an AI system treats people equitably. This becomes especially important when AI is used in hiring, lending, healthcare, policing, education, or other high-stakes decisions.

### Bias
Bias occurs when an AI system produces unfair outcomes, often because of biased data, flawed assumptions, or unequal social systems reflected in the data.

### Accountability
Accountability asks who is responsible for the outcomes of AI systems. Responsibility may involve developers, companies, governments, or institutions using the technology.

### Transparency
Transparency refers to how understandable an AI system is. Some AI systems make decisions in ways that are difficult for users or even developers to fully explain.

### Privacy
Privacy concerns how data is collected, stored, used, and shared. AI often depends on large datasets, which can create risks involving surveillance, consent, and misuse of personal information.

### Human Autonomy
Human autonomy means people should maintain meaningful control over decisions affecting their lives. Ethical concerns arise when AI manipulates, pressures, or replaces human choice.

### Human Dignity
Human dignity focuses on treating people as persons with worth, not just as data points, risk scores, or optimization targets.

## Why AI Ethics Matters

AI systems can affect employment, education, healthcare, law enforcement, creative work, and democratic life. Because of that, ethical reflection is necessary to guide design, regulation, and human use.

## Central Question

The central question of AI ethics is not only what AI can do, but what it ought to do.
