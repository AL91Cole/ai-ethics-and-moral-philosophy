# Case Study: Facial Recognition

Facial recognition technology is one of the clearest examples of why AI ethics matters.

## The Issue

Facial recognition systems are used to identify or verify people based on their facial features. These systems have been used in law enforcement, airports, phones, schools, retail spaces, and public surveillance.

## Ethical Concerns

### Privacy
Facial recognition can collect and identify people without meaningful consent. This raises major concerns about surveillance and civil liberties.

### Bias
Studies and public debate have shown that facial recognition systems may perform less accurately for certain demographic groups. This creates a serious risk when the technology is used in policing or security contexts.

### Accountability
If a system misidentifies someone and causes harm, responsibility can become unclear. Developers, vendors, institutions, and users may all share responsibility.

### Human Rights
The use of facial recognition in public spaces can create a chilling effect, where people feel watched, tracked, or less free to speak and act openly.

## Ethical Analysis

From a utilitarian view, facial recognition may be defended as useful for safety or convenience. From a deontological view, the technology may still be morally problematic if it violates privacy or treats people as objects of constant monitoring.

## Conclusion

Facial recognition shows how AI can create benefits and risks at the same time. Ethical evaluation is necessary to decide when, whether, and under what limits such technology should be used.
